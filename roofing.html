Roofing

Roof Damage

Being your home's first line of defense, weather takes a tool on your roof and damage can happen withour warning. The weather takes a toll on your roof throughout the year, and roof damage can happen without warning. Debris can reach your roof when being blown by wind or by overhanging trees and even small animals. The debris can get caught and stuck in the gutters or shingles and impede the flow of watter, sending water under the roof shingles into your home's basement, attic or foundation.

Aside from weather damage, debris can also create a fire hazard. Just a small ember from your fireplace or the neighbor's fire pit can quickly ignite dry leaves or twigs on your roof, even if your shingles are fire-retardant.

Maintenance

Maintenance need not be ignored since small problems with a roof can lead to very costly repairs. Taking the time to maintain your roof at least twice a year is well worth the effort and can save you thousands of dollars in advance.

Professional Repairs

When you notice damage to your roof, immediately call a professional. Even though you may be able to repair minor things, it is highly recommended to have a professional inspect your roof since damage might be worse than you thought.
